My Personal experience of Tanzania

Prior to this trip, I had never been to Africa before. I come from the cold north, I was raised in Sweden. I am used to the cold weathers and a lot of snow. I lived in a country for more than 20 years, where one half of the year is dark, and the other half of the year the sun is almost always up.

I recently moved to Austria on the southern mountainside of Salzburg, where my mother had recently moved. She lived in a small village with much tourism all year round. I very quickly got a job at newly opened restaurant looking for service staff.

With the village located at 902 meters above sea level, the climate was in my experience not much different from Sweden, although, not as terribly icy cold. So, I was very much used to it, unlike my significant other: a woman I met at a voluntary theater group in Salzburg, called LaborX, which does fun theater acting activities every Monday. She had traveled to Austria to do her Masters in Theater, and this is where I met her. A couple of months into our relationship, it became an idea to go visit her home country together. On the 13th of April, it was take off!

Arrival in Tanzania

After a long flight, I finally touched down at Julius Nyerere International Airport. The air was filled with a unique blend of humidity and excitement. As I made my way through customs, the anticipation of exploring a new country kept my spirits high. My first stop was Bagamoyo, a town with a name that translates to "Lay Down Your Heart," reflecting its historical significance as a trading port during the 19th century.

Interesting Facts About Bagamoyo

Bagamoyo, once the capital of German East Africa, is now a sleepy town with remnants of its historical past. It was a major port and commercial center, especially during the slave trade era. The town is also known for its role in the spread of Christianity in East Africa, with the establishment of several missions.
